SAF-T, an initiative led the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD), aims to standardize the elements, structure and format of accounting data as well as the exchange of such data.

From a Norway point of view these are the 5 basic issues that you need to be aware of :

  1. Governments are adopting methods to enable them to conduct audits remotely in a more effective manner and combat fraud in almost real time , by detecting indirect tax errors and weaknesses in taxpayersâ€™ accounting systems and controls.
  2. There are subtle differences in the way SAF-T is being implemented in these countries. For example, in Norway, SAF-T is required only in the case of a tax audit, while in other countries all VAT registered organisations must submit a SAF-T file on a monthly or quarterly basis with different data.
  3. 2017 data may already be requested in SAF-T format by authorities.
  4. In Norway, much like the other countries, the move to SAF-T will require businesses to make significant changes to their IT systems and internal processes , and rapidly respond to ever changing eGovernment legislation.
  5. Are you eligible ? All companies that maintain accounting records electronically and that are subject to Norwegian accountin rules will be required to submit SAF-T files once the law goes into effect - except those with less NOK 5 million in turnover or fewer than 600 documents a year, but there are exceptions to this.
